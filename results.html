Who are they?
Demographics data

Numerous women replied to the survey, showing that
they are well present in the value chain of the Copernicus Programme,
they want to be visible and express themselves.

Their response they gave in the survey show us that:
they are proud of their jobs
they perceive that there is a gender bias in their work place
they proposed solutions and tools to make Copernicus more gender-balanced

How they identify regarding to gender

How old are they

The majority of the women surveyed are in their thirties, 20% being younger (under 30 years old) and 40% older (over 40 years old).

What is their highest educational degree

What is their domain of expertise

They have a high educational level (30% Master and 67% Doctorate), mainly in the field of STEM (Natural sciences, mathematics and statistics; Engineering, manufacturing and construction; Information and Communication Technologies). They often have multi-disciplinary skills (30%). The “other” field is very diversified, including Business, administration and law; Social sciences, journalism and information; Agriculture, forestry, fisheries and veterinary)

Percentage of women students the year they graduated

In what level of the value chain are they using Copernicus

The big majority of the women taking the survey are involved in various levels of the value chain (94%) from upstream to dowstream and in the use of Copernicus data and products. The most cited level is the Use of Copernicus data (42% of citation density). Many of them (75%) are involved both in the Copernicus Downstream services development and the use of Copernicus data and services for different thematic applications (75%).
These results show that most of them work in the operational and downstream services, work close to the users.

and career

The majority of the women taking the survey are proud of their job in Copernicus with a global level of satisfaction of 3,8/5. Moreover, they want to talk about it: more than half of the women surveyed wanted to express themselves on the accomplishment they are the most proud of in the Copernicus/EO/GI domain.
There levels of technical skills and expertise and are also high (respectively 3,42 and 3,24/5).
However, their position in the hierarchy is quite low, with an average level of 2,8/5



Facilitators

Family support and Satisfaction in the job are the main existing facilitators. Trust and acknowledgement of their participation in Copernicus could help them and the programme.



Barriers

A gender bias exists in the Copernicus ecosystem.
Many struggle to find a good balance between their private lives and professional careers.
Gender stereotypes are still well anchored in society and have been often internalised by women.
It is important for women to invert the patriarchy paradigm by acquiring more self confidence and by creating strong links among each other.
